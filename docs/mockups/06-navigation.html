Implementation

Redirect Strategy:

  • All redirects should be 301 (permanent) to preserve SEO value
  • Implement in Next.js next.config.js redirects array
  • Track redirect hits in analytics to measure impact
  • Add proper anchor sections on target pages (e.g., /about#contact, /about#platform)

Why these mappings:

  • /volunteer → /join: Volunteering is a membership action
  • /contact → /about: Contact info lives on about page
  • /platform → /about: Platform is part of the mission
  • /press → /blog: Press releases are blog content
  • /resolutions → /about: Resolutions are part of platform
  • /endorsements → /scorecards: Scored legislators replace endorsement lists

Design Decisions

  • Public nav: Three intentional groups with dividers: The vertical dividers create visual "zones" that guide the eye. Zone 1 (core) is what the organization is about. Zone 2 (content) is for staying informed. Zone 3 (conversion) is for taking the membership step. This mirrors a classic marketing funnel: Awareness > Interest > Action.
  • Scorecards and Action Center promoted to primary nav: These are the most unique and engaging features of the RLC website. Burying them (or not having them in nav at all) means most visitors never find them. Promoting them to primary nav with bolder weight signals "this is what we're about."
  • "Join Now" as gold CTA button: The gold button breaks the text-only pattern and draws the eye as the final nav item. Gold over red because "Join" is a positive aspirational action, while red is reserved for urgency. The CTA placement at the end of the nav is the natural endpoint of left-to-right scanning.
  • Member nav: Action Center in gold: Even within the authenticated area, the engagement tools should be the most visually prominent. Gold highlight on "Action Center" creates a persistent visual invitation to participate. This is the key difference between a member portal that feels like an account settings page vs. one that feels like a mission command center.
  • Footer organized by user intent (4 columns): "About" (learn), "Take Action" (engage), "Community" (connect), "Get Involved" (commit). Every link maps to a clear user goal. The new links (Action Center, Scorecards, Social Media, Partner With Us) fill gaps where users had no path to key destinations.
  • 301 redirects for broken links: Permanent redirects preserve any existing SEO value from inbound links. The redirect-to targets were chosen based on the closest content match and most useful destination for visitors who might have bookmarked or linked to the old URLs.
