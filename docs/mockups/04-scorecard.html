Design Decisions

  • Share buttons in header AND per-row: Two sharing contexts -- share the entire scorecard (header buttons) or share a specific legislator's score (row buttons). This enables both "look at this scorecard" and "look at how YOUR rep voted" sharing behaviors.
  • Party-colored avatar borders: Red ring for Republicans, blue ring for Democrats. Instant party identification without reading the party column. Works for colorblind users because it's redundant with the text badge.
  • Three-tier score colors (green/orange/red): A/B grades get green bars, C grades get orange, D/F get red. The visual gradient makes it trivially easy to scan and identify outliers in either direction.
  • Champion badge (gold) and Needs Improvement badge (red): Gold star for scores above 85% creates aspiration and shareability ("Share your Champion"). Red warning for scores below 40% creates accountability and urgency. Both are designed to be screenshot-friendly for social media.
  • Sticky filter bar: Remains visible while scrolling through hundreds of legislators. The combination of dropdowns + search covers both browsing and targeted lookup behaviors.
  • Mobile card layout: Tables are notoriously bad on mobile. At 768px breakpoint, rows become stacked cards with the score bar prominently displayed. The contact and share buttons become full-width tap targets.
