Collapsed Behavior

  • Sidebar collapses to 64px wide at narrow viewport or via toggle
  • Only icons visible; labels appear as tooltips on hover
  • Dividers maintain group boundaries without labels
  • Active indicator (blue left border) remains visible
  • Hover tooltips appear to the right with 4px offset
  • Transition is animated (200ms ease) for smooth expansion

Design Decisions

  • 5 groups instead of 10 flat items: Reduces cognitive load from "scan 10 items" to "identify 1 of 5 categories, then pick from 1-3 items." This is an application of Miller's Law -- chunking information into manageable groups.
  • Section headers in muted uppercase: Provide hierarchy without competing visually with the navigation items themselves. The small size and low opacity make them scannable landmarks, not distractions.
  • Blue left border for active state: Chosen over background highlight alone because it provides a strong visual anchor at the left edge where the eye naturally scans in a vertical list. The blue matches the brand primary color.
  • Notification badges (red dots with counts): Surface actionable items directly in the navigation. "Members: 12" might mean 12 pending approvals. "Scorecards: 3" might mean 3 needing review. Reduces the need to check each section.
  • Dark sidebar (#1A1F2E): Creates clear separation between navigation and content area. Dark backgrounds reduce visual competition with the main content and are standard in admin interfaces.
  • Collapsed view preserves group dividers: Even at 64px, the thin divider lines maintain the grouping mental model. Users who learn the groups in expanded view can navigate by position in collapsed view.
